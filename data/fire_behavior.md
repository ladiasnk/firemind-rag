# Wildfire Behavior and Spread

## The Fire Triangle
Every fire needs three elements to burn:
1. **Fuel** — anything combustible: grass, shrubs, trees, structures
2. **Heat** — enough thermal energy to sustain combustion
3. **Oxygen** — air to feed the reaction

Remove any one element and the fire goes out. This is the basis of all firefighting strategy.

## How Fire Spreads
Fire spreads through three mechanisms:

### Radiation
Heat radiates outward in all directions from the flame. This pre-heats nearby fuels, drying them out and preparing them to ignite even before the flame front arrives.

### Convection
Hot gases and embers rise in a column above the fire. In large fires, this convection column can reach the upper atmosphere, generating its own wind patterns and even thunderstorms (pyrocumulonimbus clouds).

### Spotting
Wind carries burning embers (firebrands) ahead of the main fire front — sometimes miles away. These land and start new "spot fires," which is why wildfires can leap highways, rivers, and firebreaks.

## Topography and Fire
Fire behaves differently depending on terrain:

- **Uphill runs**: Fire climbs slopes much faster than it moves on flat ground. A fire on a 30-degree slope can move 4x faster than on flat terrain because the slope pre-heats fuel above it.
- **Chimneys and saddles**: Terrain features that funnel and accelerate wind create dangerous fire runs.
- **Aspect**: South-facing slopes in the Northern Hemisphere receive more sun, dry out faster, and carry more flammable vegetation.

## Wind Effects
Wind is the most dynamic and dangerous factor in fire behavior:
- Supplies fresh oxygen to the fire
- Dries out fuels ahead of the fire
- Pushes the flame angle forward, increasing contact with unburned fuel
- Carries spotting embers

The Santa Ana winds in Southern California and the Diablo winds in Northern California are infamous for driving catastrophic fire runs — sometimes moving faster than people can escape.

## Fire Intensity vs. Fire Severity
- **Fire intensity**: The rate of energy release during burning (a firefighting concern)
- **Fire severity**: The effect the fire has on the ecosystem and soil (an ecological concern)

High-intensity fires can sterilize soil, kill tree root systems, and set the stage for erosion and debris flows when rain eventually returns.

## Spotting Distance Records
During the 2019-2020 Australian Black Summer fires, embers were documented traveling over 30 kilometers ahead of the fire front — making containment nearly impossible.
